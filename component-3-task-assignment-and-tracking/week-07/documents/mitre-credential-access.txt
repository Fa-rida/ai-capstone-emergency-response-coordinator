==================================================
CREDENTIAL ACCESS
==================================================

Technique: Brute Force

Description:
Adversaries may use brute force techniques to gain access to accounts when passwords are unknown or when password hashes have been obtained. Attackers systematically guess passwords using repetitive authentication attempts against online services or offline credential datasets.

Brute force attacks may occur during different phases of an intrusion and are often combined with techniques such as OS Credential Dumping, Password Policy Discovery, or External Remote Services. If attackers encounter location-based access restrictions, they may alter their infrastructure to match the victim’s geographic location.

Procedure Examples:
- Sandworm Team used scripts to attempt RPC authentication against multiple hosts.
- Agrius conducted brute force activities through SMB.
- APT28 performed brute force attacks to obtain credentials.
- APT38 used brute force techniques when passwords were unknown.
- APT39 used Ncrack to reveal credentials.

--------------------------------------------------

Technique: Credential Dumping

Description:
Adversaries may dump credentials from operating system caches, memory, or security structures to obtain account login information and credential material such as password hashes or plaintext passwords.

Stolen credentials can then be used for lateral movement and access to restricted resources. Many attackers use publicly available tools such as Mimikatz, though custom credential dumping tools also exist.

Procedure Examples:
- APT28 deployed Mimikatz and custom password retrieval tools.
- APT32 used GetPassword_x64 to harvest credentials.
- APT39 used different versions of Mimikatz to obtain credentials.
- Axiom conducted credential dumping activities.
- BlackByte used Mimikatz and Cobalt Strike to dump credentials from victim systems.

--------------------------------------------------

Technique: Keylogging

Description:
Adversaries may log user keystrokes to intercept credentials and sensitive information typed by users. Keylogging is commonly used when credential dumping techniques are ineffective. Attackers may need to monitor systems for extended periods before successfully capturing credentials.

Methods of keylogging include:
- Hooking API callbacks used for processing keystrokes
- Reading raw keystroke data from hardware buffers
- Windows Registry modifications
- Custom drivers
- Modifying operating system images

Attackers may also clear browser cookies to force users to reauthenticate and enter credentials again.

Procedure Examples:
- Sandworm Team used the BlackEnergy keylogger plugin during the 2015 Ukraine Electric Power Attack.
- ADVSTORESHELL performed keylogging.
- Agent Tesla logged keystrokes on victim machines.
- Ajax Security Team used custom malware to record keystrokes.
- AppleSeed used GetKeyState and GetKeyboardState to capture keystrokes.
